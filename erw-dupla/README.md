# Reanalysis 02 — Dupla et al. 2025 (enhanced rock weathering, basalt field trial)

Dupla X, Bertagni MB, Grand S. "Three Years of Field Trials Indicate a Sustained Enhanced Rock
Weathering Signal with Limited CO2 Removal." *Environ. Sci. Technol.* 59(48):25751-25764 (2025).
doi:10.1021/acs.est.5c09820. Open access, CC-BY.

## The anomaly (worth reanalyzing)

Basalt powder (20 t/ha) on three Swiss vineyards over ~1000 days removed only **~100 ± 30 kg
CO2/ha/yr** (10-30x below model/lab upper bounds). But porewater **sodium was 3x higher** in
treated plots — unambiguous proof that basalt dissolution occurred — while the standard MRV
proxies (pH, EC, DIC, Ca, Mg) showed **no significant increase**. Dissolution is happening; the
alkalinity it should release is not showing up where the whole ERW-MRV industry looks for it.

## The first-principles angle

Use Na as a conserved dissolution tracer. Given the specific basalt's mineral stoichiometry, the
measured 3x Na excess implies a known release of Ca/Mg/alkalinity. Reconstruct the charge/mass
balance and partition the "missing" alkalinity between:
- cation exchange onto vineyard clay / organic matter (base-poor, tilled, fertilized soil)
- plant uptake
- lateral / vertical export below the sampling depth
- simply below the detection/noise floor of pH and DIC probes in a noisy agricultural matrix

Outcome either way is a citable correction to how ERW performance is measured in cropland:
"removal is under-measured by proxy choice" or a rigorous confirmation of the pessimistic read.
